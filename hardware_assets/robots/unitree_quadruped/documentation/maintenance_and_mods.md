# Maintenance and Modifications Log

This document tracks various maintenance activities, hardware modifications, and experimental hardware setups for the Unitree Go2 robots.

## Top Cover Removal

-   **Go2 Air:** The top cover can be removed by undoing the four M3 screws on top and two larger screws on the back. This allows for internal observation and photography.
-   **Go2 Pro:** Attempts to remove the top cover using the same screw method were unsuccessful. The strap motor area seems to hold it down, possibly affixed from underneath in an unknown way. Further investigation is needed if access is required.

## Platform Mounting Hardware

Experiments for mounting a platform on the robot's back using the four top screw holes:

-   **Initial Attempt:** Standoffs with a 6mm M3 screw underneath the pylon/post part. The screw was not deep enough to engage the holding thread.
-   **Ordered Hardware:**
    *   20mm M3 screws
    *   25mm M3 screws
    *   10mm high long hex nuts (M3 thread)
-   **Successful Configuration:** Using a 10mm high long hex nut with a 20mm M3 screw.
    *   The 20mm screw, when tightened fully with the 10mm nut, leaves only about 2mm of thread exposed above the nut for platform attachment.
    *   This might require a thin platform, a platform with counter-sunk holes, or a strap mechanism from the post top to the platform.
    *   A 25mm or 30mm M3 screw might provide more thread engagement for thicker platforms or additional washers. The 20mm screw barely holds the nut if tightened all the way down.

## Leg Cleaning

-   **Observations:** Legs (Joule - Go2 Pro) were dirty. Scratches tend to remain visible in the aluminum. Stains/scratches also persist on plastic parts.
-   **Potential Cleaners:** Consider trying products like Armour All or similar plastic/vinyl cleaners to improve appearance and remove stains. Effectiveness is yet to be tested.

## Motion Arm Stabilizer Mounting

-   **Bamboo Platform:** Attempted to stick the motion arm stabilizer (presumably via suction cup or adhesive) to a bamboo platform. The bamboo is too porous, and it did not stick.
-   **Alternative Platform Material:** Likely need to use a smoother, non-porous material like the 1/4" x 6" x 12" aluminum plates.
    *   Considering the standoff height for the main platform, a 1/8" thick aluminum plate might be a better choice for the motion arm stabilizer if direct mounting is preferred, to minimize overall height.
