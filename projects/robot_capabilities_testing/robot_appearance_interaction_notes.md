# Robot Appearance and Interaction Notes

This document records observations related to how the robot's physical appearance affects interactions, particularly with animals and humans.

## Googly Eyes Experiment

-   **Context:** Noticed that dogs would often bark when testing the robot.
-   **Modification:** Googly eyes were added to the robot.
-   **Observed Effect on Dogs:**
    *   There appeared to be a significant difference in how alarmed dogs were.
    *   Dogs now often seem curious rather than alarmed.
    *   With the googly eyes and a basic gait, dogs seem to treat the robot more like a strange dog.
    *   They were less worried, barked less than previously, and were more likely to approach the robot.
-   **Untested:** The effect of googly eyes has not yet been observed with the fast AI gait on the Go2 Pro (Joule).

-   **Human Reaction:**
    *   The first time someone called the robot "cute" was when the googly eyes were on.

## Implications
-   Simple aesthetic modifications like adding googly eyes can have a notable impact on human and animal perception and interaction with the robot.
-   This could be valuable for improving social acceptance or reducing anxiety in human-robot or animal-robot interactions.
